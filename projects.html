Hackathon Projects


Myodrone

  • Use Myo armband to control AR Parrot drone. Photos and videos taken by the drone are accessed by the Intel Edison microcontroller & forwarded to a web app, where they can be easily viewed across multiple platforms.
  • Top 30 out of 300+ teams at PennApps Spring 2015.
  • MyoDrone on Github

RoboHome

  • Internet of Things (IoT) themed hardware project: a model home that has components such as lighting, door, TV, and shower. Each of these home components were connected to a microcontroller and bluetooth module; they were controlled using a variety of mobile devices: smartphone, pebble watch, myo armband, and laptop.
  • Used Parse backend to persistently store data and communicate with our Android mobile app for displaying statuses of the household components (e.g. house lights are 'on'), and controlling these components. We also utilized 3D printing to create our shower handle and our Robohome resident, Pikachu.
  • First Place out of 200+ competitors at IDEA Hacks 2016
  • RoboHome on Github

Alexa MD

  • Hardware IoT project with three components: Amazon Echo will prompt the user with various treatment options which are a version of exposure therapy. The user will then engage in virtual reality treatment via Oculus Rift by re-enacting various situations that may usually cause them anxiety or distress. Treatments will incrementally lessen the user’s anxieties; they can use the Leap Motion to engage in another dimension of treatment when they are ready to move to the next step.
  • When the treatment session has concluded, Alexa M.D. will dispense the user’s prescribed medication through an automated medicine dispenser, powered by the Intel Edison. This ensures users take appropriate dosages while also encouraging them to go through their treatment session before taking their medication.
  • Won Best Use of Amazon Echo and Best Health Wearable Prize at TreeHacks 2016
  • Alexa MD on Github

MyoMessenger

  • Hardware driving safety project with two components: The first uses Myo technology and allows you to send common text messages to any person on a predetermined emergency list (e.g. text 'I will call you back later' to Mom) using hand gestures. This allows you to communicate your whereabouts safely with friends and family without needing to deviate your eyes from the road. The second safety feature uses the Muse headband’s accelerometer to detect when a driver’s head starts bobbing in exhaustion or fatigue. When the driver is detected to be falling asleep, it will send an audiovisual signal for the driver to wake up.
  • Top 4 out of 1000+ competitors at CalHacks 2015.
  • MyoMessenger on Github

HappyHour

  • Pebble Time application for safety that allows users to keep track of how many drinks they’ve had, calculate their BAC in real time, and give them the option to call an Uber to their location at the click of a button. Won best use of Uber API prize; featured on Devpost‘s top projects.
  • HappyHour on Github

SafeSteps

  • Pebble application for safety that allows users to send a call + SMS message to as many friends as they would like simultaneously at a click of a button. The call includes a voice message saying that the user is in trouble, and the SMS message also includes the address of where the user is located.
  • SafeSteps on Github

ScenicJogger

  • Web Application that empowers citizens to explore historical landmarks in their cities by intelligently recommending jogging routes that satisfies the user’s desired running distance while routing around historical landmarks. In addition, the app allows users to see a hyperlapse of their route by using Google Maps Street View API and stitching the street view images together. Finally, a Twilio node-js server allows users to send their desired route to their phone for future access when they are on their jog.
  • ScenicJogger on Github
